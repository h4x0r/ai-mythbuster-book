# Presentation Script: Myth #4 - "Just Prompt and Go"

**Section Duration:** 4 minutes
**Slides:** 15, 16, 17 (Myth → Reality → So What pattern)
**Energy Level:** Practical, problem-solving
**Audience Interaction:** Relatable frustration moment

---

## SLIDE 15: The Myth

### Visual Description
- **Layout:** Full-screen visual with text overlay
- **Background:** Magic wand with sparkles/stars emanating
- **Color scheme:** Whimsical, bright (purple/gold sparkles)
- **Text:**
  - Top: "Myth #4" (36pt, white with glow)
  - Center: "It Just Works" (84pt, bold, white with glow)

### Speaker Script

**[Advance to slide]**

"Myth number four."

**[Gesture to magic wand visual]**

"It just works."

**[Pause, knowing smile]**

"You've seen the demos, right?"

**[Mimicking typing]**

"Someone types a question, gets a perfect answer, ships it. Easy!"

**[Change tone - relatable frustration]**

"So you try it yourself. You type 'Write a marketing plan'..."

**[Gesture broadly, frustrated]**

"...and you get Generic Template number 4,000-something. Completely useless."

**[Pause, look at audience]**

"Sound familiar?"

**[Nod, acknowledging shared experience]**

**[Direct]**

"The problem isn't you. The problem is expecting AI to read your mind."

**[Click to next slide]**

---

### Timing Notes
- **Total time on slide:** 35-40 seconds
- **Key pause:** After "Sound familiar?" - let them connect to their own frustration
- **Relatable moment:** The "useless template" experience resonates

### Delivery Notes
- **Tone:** Start whimsical (magic wand), shift to shared frustration
- **Validation:** They've experienced this - acknowledge it
- **Setup:** "Read your mind" sets up next slide perfectly

---

## SLIDE 16: The Reality

### Visual Description
- **Layout:** Two-funnel comparison
- **Background:** White/light gray
- **Title:** "Garbage In, Garbage Out" (60pt, blue, top)

**Visual:** Two side-by-side funnels/pipelines

**Left Funnel (Vague Input):**
- Top (input): "Write report" (small, vague)
- Funnel: Messy, chaotic visualization
- Bottom (output): Generic mess/gibberish

**Right Funnel (Structured Input):**
- Top (input): "Write Q3 sales report: intro, data, trends, conclusion for board meeting, 1 page" (detailed, structured)
- Funnel: Clean, organized visualization
- Bottom (output): Clean, organized document

**Arrows or labels:**
- Left: ❌ "Vague = Useless"
- Right: ✓ "Specific = Useful"

### Speaker Script

**[Slide advances]**

"Reality: Garbage in, garbage out."

**[Point to left funnel]**

"When you type 'Write report' - AI has to guess."

**[Enumerate with frustration]**

"What kind of report?
For whom?
About what?
How long?
What format?"

**[Gesture broadly]**

"So it defaults to the most common pattern it's seen. Which is generic and useless."

**[Point to right funnel]**

"But when you give it structure..."

**[Read the detailed input]**

"'Write Q3 sales report: intro, data, trends, conclusion, for board meeting, one page.'"

**[Show connection with hands]**

"Now AI knows what pattern to complete."

**[Direct, clear]**

"Same AI. Different results."

**[Emphasize]**

"The difference? How much structure YOU provide."

**[Pause, let it land]**

**[Shift to empowering]**

"This isn't AI failing. This is you being in control."

**[Click to next slide]**

---

### Timing Notes
- **Total time on slide:** 60-70 seconds
- **Key pauses:**
  - After "Garbage in, garbage out": 1-2 seconds (let principle land)
  - After "Same AI. Different results": 2 seconds (contrast moment)
  - After "This is you being in control": 2 seconds (empowerment)

### Delivery Notes
- **Tone:** Problem → solution → empowerment
- **Contrast:** Make vague sound frustrating, specific sound satisfying
- **Ownership:** "YOU provide" - emphasize their control

## SLIDE 17: So What

### Visual Description
- **Layout:** Before/After comparison
- **Background:** White/light gray
- **Title:** "Engineer Your Prompts" (60pt, bold, blue, top)

**Visual:** Two-column comparison

**Before (Bad Prompt):**
```
"Analyze data"
```
- Small, lonely text
- Red X or error symbol

**After (Good Prompt):**
```
"Analyze Q3 sales data.
Find top 3 trends.
Explain each in one sentence.
Include supporting numbers."
```
- Structured, detailed
- Green checkmark

**Subtitle:** "Be specific. AI will follow."

### Speaker Script

**[Slide advances]**

"So what do you actually do?"

**[Pause]**

"Engineer your prompts."

**[Point to before]**

"Don't do this: 'Analyze data.'"

**[Shake head]**

"Analyze WHAT data? Find WHAT insights? In WHAT format?"

**[Point to after]**

"Do this."

**[Read it clearly]**

"'Analyze Q3 sales data. Find top 3 trends. Explain each in one sentence. Include supporting numbers.'"

**[Contrast with hands - small vs big]**

"Notice the difference?"

**[Enumerate the elements]**

"Specific task: What data.
Clear goal: Top 3 trends.
Format requirement: One sentence each.
Completeness check: Include numbers."

**[Direct, practical]**

"Prompt engineering isn't magic."

**[Emphasize each word]**

"It's. Being. Specific."

**[Pause]**

**[Empowering tone]**

"You tell AI exactly what you want. AI delivers."

**[Lighter, transitioning]**

"Be specific, get results. That simple."

**[Pause]**

"One more myth to bust..."

**[Click to next slide]**

---

### Timing Notes
- **Total time on slide:** 60-70 seconds
- **Key pauses:**
  - After "Engineer your prompts": 1-2 seconds
  - After "It's. Being. Specific.": 2 seconds (staccato emphasis)
  - After "That simple": 1 second

### Delivery Notes
- **Tone:** Instructional but accessible
- **Read the good prompt:** Clearly, so they understand the structure
- **Staccato:** "It's. Being. Specific." - break it up for impact
- **Simplify:** Don't overcomplicate - this is straightforward

## Section Wrap-Up: Practical Skill Building

**Purpose of Myth #4:**
- Addresses common frustration (generic AI outputs)
- Teaches core skill (prompt engineering)
- Empowers audience (you control quality through inputs)
- Provides actionable framework (be specific)

**Energy Check:** By end of this section, audience should:
- ✓ Understand why their early AI attempts were disappointing
- ✓ Know the solution (structured prompts)
- ✓ Feel capable of improving their results
- ✓ Have concrete example to try

**Transition:** Now that they know how to engineer prompts, Myth #5 reveals the danger of vibe coding (fast demos vs production reality).
